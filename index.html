Why Topinambur (Jerusalem Artichoke)?

Topinambur, also known as Jerusalem artichoke (Helianthus tuberosus), is a powerhouse of nutrients. Its rich amino acid, vitamin, mineral and inulin content provides therapeutic and preventive benefits not only for humans but also for animal feed applications.

A True Wholefood Prebiotic

  • Minimal natural sugars

This composition makes it a true wholefood prebiotic and functional healthy food ingredient.

History of Jerusalem Artichoke

Although the name "Jerusalem artichoke" suggests an exotic fusion of geography and cuisine, it is actually a linguistic accident. These plants - also known as sunchokes or topinambur - are neither true artichokes nor do they originate from Jerusalem. Instead, they are the underground tubers of Helianthus tuberosus, a tall, hardy species native to North America and closely related to the common sunflower.

Indigenous peoples such as the Cree, Huron, and other Native American tribes cultivated and consumed these tubers for centuries before the arrival of Europeans, valuing them as a reliable, nutritious staple.

The misleading name emerged through translation and culinary association. When the plant was brought to Europe in the early 1600s, it attracted attention for its artichoke-like flavor. The Italian word "girasole" (turning toward the sun) was distorted into "Jerusalem," while the French "topinambour" became another common European name.

Prebiotics for Your Health

What Are Prebiotics?

Prebiotics are substrates selectively used by beneficial microorganisms to provide health benefits. In simple terms, they are food for the friendly microbes that live in and on our bodies.

Health Benefits of Prebiotics

Prebiotics such as inulin from Jerusalem artichoke or topinambur can help:

  • Improve digestion and bowel regularity
  • Support immune function
  • Enhance mineral absorption
  • Regulate appetite, energy balance, and glucose metabolism

Prebiotics and the Microbiome

Each person carries around 38 trillion microbes that play essential roles in metabolic, digestive, and immune health. Prebiotics, which the body's enzymes cannot digest, serve as nourishment for these beneficial intestinal microbes.

Dietary Sources of Prebiotics

A diverse diet of plant-based whole foods - including fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, seeds - is key to increasing prebiotic intake. Examples:

  • Inulin and FOS from onions, garlic, and Jerusalem artichokes
  • Resistant starch from cooled cooked potatoes
  • Polyphenols in many plant foods

Western diets often lack sufficient prebiotics, making supplements or prebiotic-rich foods like topinambur especially valuable.

Applications of Topinambur and Inulin

One of the most valuable qualities of topinambur is its high content of inulin - up to 80% of its dry substance content. Pectin content can reach 11% of dry weight. Consuming topinambur as a healthy food ingredient supports digestive, cardiovascular, and liver health.
